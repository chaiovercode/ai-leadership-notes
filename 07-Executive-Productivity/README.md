## 1. The Mental Model: Chief of Staff

Stop thinking of ChatGPT as a **Software**.
Start thinking of it as a **Harvard Grad Intern** who is:
1.  **Incredibly Smart** (Read everything on the internet).
2.  **Incredibly Fast** (Types at light speed).
3.  **Incredibly Naive** (Has no common sense and lies sometimes).
4.  **Available 24/7** (Does not sleep).

### How Do You Manage an Intern Like That?

-   You do not say: "Write a strategy." (They will write generic fluff).
-   You say: "Here is our Q3 data. Here is the competitor pricing. Draft a memo proposing a 10% price cut, listing 3 pros and 3 cons."

### The Golden Rule of Executive Prompting

Give **Context**, not just **Commands**.
"Treat me like the CEO. Be brief. Be ruthless."

### The Three Cs Framework

Every executive prompt should include:

| Component | Description | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Context** | Who you are. What the situation is. | "I am the CEO of a SaaS company with $20M ARR." |
| **Constraint** | The rules. Format. Length. Style. | "Keep it under 200 words. Use bullet points." |
| **Command** | What you want done. Specific action. | "Summarize the top 3 risks in this contract." |

### Bad vs. Good Executive Prompts

| Bad Prompt | Good Prompt |
| :--- | :--- |
| "Help me with my presentation." | "I am presenting to our board next week about expanding to Europe. Review this draft and identify weak arguments. Be critical." |
| "Summarize this document." | "Summarize this 50-page contract for me. Focus on liability, termination clauses, and payment terms. Use a table format." |
| "Write an email to John." | "Write a firm but respectful email to John declining his partnership proposal. We cannot do it due to resource constraints. Leave the door open for future discussions." |

## 2. The Second Brain: Your Personal Knowledge Engine

Leaders drown in information. Emails, PDFs, Slack, News.
You need a "Second Brain" to remember it all.

### The "Librarian" Analogy

Imagine you have a personal Librarian who follows you everywhere.
Every time you read an article, you hand it to her. "File this."
Every time you have a shower thought, you tell her. "Write this down."

Then, when you are in a meeting 6 months later, you whisper:
*"What was that stat about customer retention we saw in that article last May?"*
She hands you the exact paragraph instantly.

### The Stack: Capture, Storage, Retrieval

**1. Capture (The Net)**

Tools that collect information automatically:
- **Readwise Reader:** Saves and highlights articles, PDFs, newsletters
- **Otter.ai:** Records and transcribes meetings automatically
- **Matter:** Saves and syncs podcast highlights
- **Voice Memos + Whisper:** Capture thoughts on the go

**2. Storage (The Library)**

Tools that organize your knowledge:
- **Notion:** Structured databases, wikis, documents
- **Obsidian:** Linked notes, personal knowledge graph
- **Roam Research:** Bidirectional links, daily notes

**3. Retrieval (The Search)**

Tools that let you query your knowledge:
- **NotebookLM:** Upload documents, ask questions, get sourced answers
- **Glean:** Enterprise search across all your company tools
- **Mem.ai:** AI-native note-taking with smart search

### The NotebookLM Workflow

This is the killer app for executives in 2025.

**Action:**
1. Upload your last 50 Board Decks into NotebookLM
2. Query: "How has our CAC trended over the last 8 quarters?"
3. Result: Instant analysis with citations to specific slides

**Power Moves:**
- Upload a competitor's 10-K and ask "What are their top 3 strategic priorities?"
- Upload meeting transcripts and ask "What did Sarah commit to delivering?"
- Upload a contract and ask "What are the escape clauses?"

No emailing the CFO. No searching through folders. Instant truth.

## 3. Decision Making: The Simulator

Leaders make bets.
AI lets you **simulate the bet** before you place the chips.

### The "Devil's Advocate" Workflow

You are in love with your new idea. Your team is too scared to tell you it sucks.
AI has no fear.

**The Prompt:**
> "I am the CEO. I plan to launch Product X. Here is the plan.
> Act as a ruthless Short-Seller investor.
> Tell me exactly why this plan will fail. Tear it apart.
> Do not be polite."

The AI will give you the "Pre-Mortem."
-   "Your margin is too thin."
-   "Competitor Y already owns this niche."
-   "The regulatory risk is high."

**Now you can fix the plan *before* you launch.**

### The "Board Member" Workflow

Before presenting to your board, simulate the conversation.

**The Prompt:**
> "You are a skeptical board member with 30 years of experience.
> I am going to present this strategy to you.
> Ask me the 5 hardest questions you would ask.
> Then, after I answer, tell me how convincing my answers were."

### The "Competitor" Workflow

**The Prompt:**
> "You are the CEO of [Competitor Name].
> You just read our leaked strategy document [paste it].
> How would you respond? What is our biggest vulnerability you would exploit?"

### The Scenario Planning Matrix

Use AI to map out scenarios:

| Scenario | Probability | Impact | Response Strategy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Competitor price cut 20% | ? | ? | ? |
| Key hire leaves | ? | ? | ? |
| Regulation changes | ? | ? | ? |
| Tech shift | ? | ? | ? |

**The Prompt:**
> "Fill in this scenario planning matrix for our business. Be realistic about probabilities. Suggest response strategies for each."

## 4. Meetings: The Scribe

If you are typing notes in a meeting, you are not listening.
If you are trying to remember what was said, you are not thinking.

**Outsource Memory.**

### The AI Scribe Workflow

1. **Record** the audio (Always tell people you are recording)
2. **Transcribe** it automatically
3. **Summarize** it: "What were the Action Items? Who owns them?"
4. **Search** it later: "What did we decide about the budget?"

| **Granola** | Mac-native. Minimalist. Privacy-focused. |

### The Day-After Magic

"Copilot, did we decide to hire the agency in that meeting last Tuesday? Or did we delay it?"

**You never lose a decision again.**

### The Prep Workflow

Before important meetings, AI can prepare you:

**The Prompt:**
> "I have a meeting with [Name] from [Company] tomorrow.
> Here is what I know about them [paste LinkedIn, previous emails, notes].
> Summarize who they are, what they care about, and suggest 3 questions I should ask."

## 5. Communications: The Mirror

You send an email. You think you sound "Assertive."
Your team thinks you sound "Angry."
This gap causes chaos.

Use AI as a **Tone Mirror**.

### The Tone Check Workflow

**The Prompt:**
> "Paste Email Draft.
> Read this email. Tell me how it sounds to the recipient.
> Does it sound collaborative or dictatorial?
> Rewrite it to be Firm but Kind."

### The Email Templates

**Declining a Request:**
> "Write a polite but firm email declining [request].
> We cannot do it because [reason].
> Offer an alternative if possible.
> Keep it under 100 words."

**Delivering Hard News:**
> "Write an email announcing [difficult news] to the team.
> Be direct but empathetic.
> Explain the why.
> End with a forward-looking message."

**Following Up:**
> "Write a follow-up email to [Name] who has not responded to my previous email.
> Nudge gently. Do not sound passive-aggressive.
> Suggest a specific next step."

### The 2 AM Rule

It saves you from sending the emotional 2 AM email that you regret at 9 AM.

Before sending any high-stakes email:
1. Draft it
2. Ask AI: "How does this sound? What could be misinterpreted?"
3. Edit
4. Sleep on it
5. Send in the morning

## 6. Research: The Analyst

Executives need information fast. But reading 100 pages is slow.
AI compresses information.

### The Briefing Workflow

**For News and Industry Updates:**
> "Summarize the top 5 AI developments from the past week that a CEO should know about.
> Focus on business implications, not technical details."

**For Competitive Intelligence:**
> "Here is [Competitor's] latest earnings call transcript.
> Summarize their strategy in 3 bullet points.
> Identify the biggest threat to us."

**For Market Research:**
> "Here is a 50-page market research report.
> Summarize the key findings in a one-page executive summary.
> Include the data that surprised you most."

Perplexity Pro is the best research tool for executives in 2025.

Unlike ChatGPT, it:
- Searches the web in real-time

**Use It For:**

## 7. Writing: The Ghostwriter

Leaders spend hours writing. Memos. Emails. Presentations.
AI handles the first draft. You handle the last draft.

### The 80/20 Rule of AI Writing

AI writes the first 80% (structure, content, initial draft).
You write the last 20% (voice, nuance, judgment calls).

### The Writing Workflow

**Step 1: Outline**
> "Create an outline for a memo about [Topic].
> Include: context, analysis, recommendation, next steps."

**Step 2: Draft**
> "Expand this outline into a full memo.
> Write in a direct, executive tone.
> Keep it under 2 pages."

**Step 3: Critique**
> "Read this draft. Tell me what is weak, unclear, or missing."

**Step 4: Polish**
> "Rewrite this to be 30% shorter without losing meaning."

### Writing for Different Audiences

**For the Board:**
> "Rewrite this for board members. Lead with the recommendation. Support with data. Keep it formal."

**For Employees:**
> "Rewrite this for all-hands communication. Make it inspiring and accessible. Avoid jargon."

**For Investors:**
> "Rewrite this for investors. Focus on growth metrics and market opportunity. Be confident but credible."

## 10. Workflow Automation: The Invisible Team

Beyond individual tasks, AI can automate entire workflows.
The goal: work that happens without you.

### The "Invisible Assistant" Concept

For executives, AI agents reduce manual work, summarize meetings, manage schedules, prioritize communications, and provide real-time insights.

An AI executive assistant handles after-hours scheduling, manages time zones for global meetings, and protects personal time while maintaining professional commitments.

### Common Executive Automations

| Workflow | Automation |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Morning Briefing** | AI summarizes overnight emails, news, and calendar |
| **Meeting Follow-up** | AI sends notes and action items within 1 hour |
| **Expense Reports** | AI extracts data from receipts and drafts reports |
| **Weekly Review** | AI compiles metrics and drafts status update |
| **Travel Planning** | AI books flights, hotels based on preferences |

### The Delegation Test

Ask yourself: "Would I delegate this to an assistant if I had one?"
If yes, automate it with AI.

### Building Your First Automation

Start simple:
1. Pick one repetitive weekly task
2. Document the steps
3. Use a tool like Zapier + AI or Make.com
4. Run in parallel with manual process for 2 weeks
5. Trust and automate fully

## 1. AI for Personal Use

### The "Centaur" Model
Combining Human Intuition + AI Horsepower.
*   **The Blank Page Problem:** Use AI to draft the first 80% of emails, memos, or strategies.
*   **The Universal Intern:** Summarizing 50-page reports, converting meeting notes into action items, or preparing for tough negotiations.
*   **Tools:** ChatGPT Plus, Perplexity (for search), Otter.ai (meeting transcription).

---

## 2. AI for Programming

### The Great Leveler
You no longer need to know syntax to build software. You need to know *logic*.
*   **For Developers:** GitHub Copilot acts as a pair programmer, writing boilerplate code, unit tests, and documentation. Increases velocity by 30-50%.
*   **For Non-Technical Leaders:** You can now build prototypes, dashboards, and automations (SQL queries, Python scripts) using natural language.
*   **Risk:** "Code bloat" and security vulnerabilities if code is not reviewed.

## 3. AI in Customer Journey

From "Mass Marketing" to "Mass Personalization".

*   **Awareness (Marketing):** Generative AI creates thousands of ad variations (image + copy) to test what works.
*   **Consideration (Sales):** AI Agents (SDRs) research prospects and draft hyper-personalized outreach emails (no more generic spam).
*   **Support (Post-Sales):**
    *   *Old:* "Press 1 for English."
    *   *New:* AI Voice Agents that understand intent, handle refunds, and speak 50 languages fluently.

## 4. AI in Product

### AI-Enabled vs. AI-Native
*   **AI-Enabled:** Adding a "Summarize" button to Microsoft Word. It improves an existing workflow.
*   **AI-Native:** A product that couldn't exist without AI. Example: **Midjourney** (Art), **Sunno** (Music), or **Narrativ** (Storytelling).

### The Feature Trap
Don't just add a chatbot to your app. Solves a specific user problem where intelligence was the bottleneck.

## 5. AI in Finance

*   **Fraud Detection:** Traditional rules (if > $10k, flag it) miss nuance. AI analyzes behavior patterns (mouse movement, typing speed, transaction graphs) to catch fraud in real-time.
*   **Algorithmic Trading:** Analyzing alternative data sources (satellite imagery of parking lots, sentiment analysis of news) to predict stock movements.

## 6. AI in Operations and Supply Chain

*   **Predictive Maintenance:** Sensors on machines listen for "weird noises" (vibrations). AI predicts failure *weeks* before it happens, preventing downtime.
*   **Demand Forecasting:** Moving from simple trends (Sales last year + 5%) to complex modeling (Weather + Local Events + Social Media Trends).
*   **Route Optimization:** UPS/FedEx saving millions of miles by dynamically adjusting routes based on real-time traffic and AI predictions.
